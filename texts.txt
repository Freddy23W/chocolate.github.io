Chapter I — 

Dear Tomorrow



Dear Tomorrow begins at the origin: childhood. Written as a love letter to Black queer children, it offers tenderness in a world that often attempts to define and limit them before they can define themselves. The piece acknowledges how societies construct categories that quietly shape a life's possibilities.


At the same time, it is a promise—to protect, to nurture, and to remain present through the uncertainties ahead. The chapter extends beyond the self, reaching toward future generations and the longing to one day guide children through the world with care, love, and understanding.





Chapter II — 

The Black Epiphany



The Black Epiphany explores the moment of awakening when many Black individuals first recognise the inequalities embedded within society. This realisation—often experienced at a young age—brings with it a complex mix of confusion, anger, and clarity.


Archival voices from influential thinkers and activists such as Angela Davis, Bobby Seale, Audre Lorde, and Kwame Ture echo through the chapter. Their words serve as both historical memory and a reminder that the systems shaping these experiences continue to evolve rather than disappear.





Chapter III — 

Taste the Dark



Taste the Dark reflects on the sexual liberation of Black queer bodies and the tensions that exist within spaces of desire. In nightlife, techno, and underground scenes, visibility can bring both celebration and objectification.


The chapter explores how Black queer individuals often navigate attraction alongside fetishisation, validation alongside microaggressions. Within this complexity, the work asks what it means to reclaim the body—not as fantasy or projection, but as a site of autonomy, pleasure, and self-definition.





Chapter IV — 

Remember Me



Remember Me looks toward legacy and the future of Black queerness. It asks what remains after a life is lived and what messages we might hope to leave behind.


Rather than focusing on endings, the chapter imagines remembrance as a collective act—carried through community, care, and shared memory. It offers a vision rooted in hope, love, and understanding, inviting future generations to nurture and continue the possibilities created before them.
